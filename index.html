I'm a full-stack web developer striving to create accessible, useful, and enjoyable experiences. I work primarily in JavaScript and PHP, am hugely fond of React, and feel genuine excitement every time I get to wield flexbox on a layout. I'm currently with Alley solving interesting problems for publishers and media organizations.

In past professional lives, I've been a children's book editor and career counselor, though I've dabbled with code and software all my life (i.e. played Mouse Practice as a five-year-old). In 2018, I won a full scholarship through Grow with Google to complete a Front-End Nanodegree through Udacity, after finishing an initial challenge phase in the top 10% of participants. My editorial eye and problem-solving abilities drive my code and design.

When I'm not writing code, I'm probably reading, embroidering, cooking, or walking around NYC.

Notes and Snippets

January 31, 2021

My kaizen for this year is to tell someone when I've learned something new, even if that someone is my future self.

The professional development reasoning is two-fold: to practice communicating confidently, and to make a more consistent habit out of recording what I learn. To build a resource library I can reference back.

I don't envision sticky notes being the long-term format of this library. This space seems better suited to quick jottings.
